Auckland

Auckland, based around 2 large harbours, is a major city in the north of New Zealand’s
North Island. In the centre, the iconic Sky Tower has views of Viaduct Harbour,
which is full of superyachts and lined with bars and cafes. Auckland Domain, the city’s oldest park,
is based around an extinct volcano and home to the formal Wintergardens. Near Downtown, Mission Bay Beach
has a seaside promenade.

Hamilton

Ask a local Hamiltonian the number one thing you shouldn't miss in their city,
and there's a high chance they'll say the Hamilton Gardens. While traditional
botanical gardens focus on types of plants and are organized accordingly,
the Hamilton Gardens are laid out according to different kinds of garden design.
The effect is a showcase of the cultural meaning of gardens over the centuries.

Taupo

Taupo, a town near the centre of New Zealand's North Island,
is distinguished by its lakefront setting and outdoor sports ranging
from fishing to jet-boating. The vast waters of Lake Taupo, a volcanic caldera,
drain into the Huka Falls, dramatic, crystal-blue cascades reachable by hiking
and biking trails. Surrounding Taupo are hot springs like those filling the Craters of the Moon reserve.
